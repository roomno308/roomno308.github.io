**Theorem 1 (Pythagoras)** Let $a$, $b$, and $c$ denote the length of the sides
of a right triangle, i.e. of a triangle with one angle equal to 90°. Without loss of 
generality, assume that $a ≤ b ≤ c$. Then

$$a^2 + b^2 = c^2$$
